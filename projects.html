Socioviz (2014–2024): A web-based SNA tool for Twitter

Affiliation: Independent Project / Startup
2014 - 2024

For over a decade, Socioviz proudly served the research community by mapping digital conversations. Launched in 2014 as a web-based platform powered by Social Network Analysis metrics, it allowed researchers, journalists, and marketers to query the Twitter API, gather real-time data, and visualize complex networks without writing a single line of code.

The platform offered advanced features such as historical and real-time searches, emoji analytics, and the extraction of semantic and user interaction networks in standard formats like GEXF and CSV.

Reference: Zonin, A. (2025). Socioviz (2014–2024): A web-based Social Network Analysis tool for Twitter. Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17913836. 176 citations on Google Scholar.

The Italian Startup Ecosystem on Twitter

Affiliation: IBM Italia & Talent Garden
2016 - 2020

Over four consecutive editions, this research project analyzed the digital conversations of the Italian tech and startup community. Conducted in partnership with IBM Italia and Talent Garden, the study applied Social Network Analysis to millions of tweets generated by hundreds of thousands of users to understand who drives digital innovation in Italy and what they talk about.

The reports mapped the evolution of key industry trends—such as Fintech, Internet of Things (IoT), and Industry 4.0—while identifying top influencers, mapping community hubs across different cities, and analyzing demographic engagement across the national innovation landscape.

Social Data Stories for Innovazione PA

Affiliation: Innovazione PA (Soiel International)
2018

A series of data journalism features and cover stories published in the Italian magazine Innovazione PA. By combining Social Network Analysis (SNA) and semantic sentiment analysis, the articles mapped public debates, public administration updates, and breaking news trends across Italian Twitter conversations.

Key analyses included an extensive study of over 40,000 tweets regarding the challenges and ethics of Artificial Intelligence (in parallel with the AgID White Paper release), as well as semantic network visualizations exploring critical social issues such as school violence and digital transformation policies.

We Are All Nodes, Living in the Network

Affiliation: TEDxVerona February 2014

A TEDx talk introducing the history, methodology, and modern applications of Social Network Analysis (SNA). The presentation traces the mathematical roots of network theory from Leonhard Euler's graph theory to Jacob Levi Moreno's sociogram, before shifting focus to the digital age. It explores how modern SNA is applied to social media data for influencer identification, community mapping, and the semantic analysis of online conversations.

To ground these complex concepts, the talk concludes with a unique case study in educational sciences: demonstrating how kindergarten children intuitively mapped their relational structures using a ball of yarn passed from hand to hand—creating a literal, physical "web of friendship" that beautifully visualizes the core principles of networks.

The Fashion Side of Data: Social Network Analysis & Fashion

Affiliation: IBM Italia & Università Cattolica December 2013

A comprehensive research project analyzing economic and technological trends in the fashion industry through social media data. Conducted in collaboration with the IBM Italia team, Università Cattolica di Milano, and MF Fashion, the study analyzed over 112,000 tweets and millions of social interactions to map conversation networks and hashtag co-occurrences.

By applying visual analytics and Social Network Analysis (SNA), the project identified key influencers, engagement dynamics between fashion bloggers and official brands, and semantic clusters bridging fashion with technology and e-commerce platforms.

Vinitaly 2013 on Twitter: Social Network Analysis & Data Journalism

Affiliation: L'Arena di Verona & Dataninja.it June 2013

A data journalism and research project conducted in collaboration with Irene Pasquetto, analyzing the digital footprint and social media chatter during the Vinitaly 2013 international wine exhibition. The study was published as a dedicated feature in the newspaper L'Arena di Verona and showcased within Dataninja.it's research lab.

By applying Social Network Analysis (SNA) methodologies to thousands of tweets containing the organic hashtags #vinitaly and #vinitaly2013, the project mapped the interaction patterns, temporal spikes, and network structures of over 2,600 unique users. The findings tracked the engagement ratios of top active wine bloggers, official institutional accounts (@vinitalytasting), and high-profile industry influencers who drove the global digital conversation.
