Urban Life and Aging: A Social Media Perspective

Affiliation: NICA (UK Government) & Newcastle University
2024

A global research collaboration with the UK’s National Innovation Centre for Ageing (NICA), exploring how city living, human curiosity, and music intersect to impact the quality of life for aging populations. The project aimed to gather data-driven insights to help shape age-friendly, inclusive urban environments.

Over 30,000 tweets and 170,000 Instagram posts were gathered across multiple languages. By combining Social Network Analysis (gathered and mapped via Socioviz) with AI-powered topic modeling and sentiment analysis, the study mapped the universal challenges of urban affordability and mobility, while highlighting the profound cognitive and social benefits of music and lifelong curiosity for older adults.

Reference: Full insights published on NICA’s online magazine This Curious Life.

Socioviz (2014–2024): A web-based SNA tool for Twitter

Affiliation: Independent Project / Startup
2014 - 2024

For over a decade, Socioviz proudly served the research community by mapping digital conversations. Launched in 2014 as a web-based platform powered by Social Network Analysis metrics, it allowed researchers, journalists, and marketers to query the Twitter API, gather real-time data, and visualize complex networks without writing a single line of code.

The platform offered advanced features such as historical and real-time searches, emoji analytics, and the extraction of semantic and user interaction networks in standard formats like GEXF and CSV.

Reference: Zonin, A. (2025). Socioviz (2014–2024): A web-based Social Network Analysis tool for Twitter. Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17913836. 176 citations on Google Scholar.

Cyberbullying: How it's Discussed Online

Affiliation: Parole O_Stili, Dock, Joined in Tech, Sistemi Informativi
2021

In collaboration with Sistemi Informativi, Dock, Joined in Tech, and Parole O_Stili, this research project analyzed how cyberbullying is perceived and discussed across social media. Sparked by the exponential rise of online psychological violence during the 2020 pandemic lockdowns, the study tracked digital conversations to map cultural reflections and societal concerns.

The analysis gathered thousands of posts from Twitter and Instagram around Safer Internet Day 2021. By examining hashtags and keyword frequencies, the research highlighted key themes such as "social loneliness" and the urgent need for digital education in schools. The findings demonstrated how different platforms handle the topic, contrasting Twitter's focus on institutional solutions with Instagram's emphasis on emotional support and personal stories.

The Italian Startup Ecosystem on Twitter

Affiliation: IBM Italia & Talent Garden
2016 - 2020

Over four consecutive editions, this research project analyzed the digital conversations of the Italian tech and startup community. Conducted in partnership with IBM Italia and Talent Garden, the study applied Social Network Analysis to millions of tweets generated by hundreds of thousands of users to understand who drives digital innovation in Italy and what they talk about.

The reports mapped the evolution of key industry trends—such as Fintech, Internet of Things (IoT), and Industry 4.0—while identifying top influencers, mapping community hubs across different cities, and analyzing demographic engagement across the national innovation landscape.

Legend19: The Manifesto of Legendary Brands

Affiliation: Promemoria, Accurat, Lavazza, IBM Italia
June 2019

A live data visualization and sentiment analysis project during the "Legend19" event, organized by Promemoria in collaboration with Lavazza, exploring how brands and individuals transform into cultural icons.

Using IBM Watson's Natural Language capabilities, the project captured and analyzed live stage speeches and real-time Twitter conversations (#legend19). Watson identified seven different emotional tones (such as joy, sadness, and anger) sentence by sentence. These live insights were instantly transformed into a dynamic, generative data visualization created by the renowned information design studio Accurat, which was projected live behind the speakers to visually represent the emotional shape of the ongoing discourse.

Data Stories: Inspiring Copywriters through SNA

Affiliation: PlayCopy / Forum Monzani
April 2019

A highly attended keynote speech and workshop delivered at PlayCopy, Italy's premier event for professional writing and content marketing, hosted at the Forum Monzani in Modena. The talk explored the fascinating intersection of computer science and traditional humanities—a field known as Digital Humanities.

Instead of relying purely on intuition, the session demonstrated to an audience of copywriters and marketers how social media listening, Emoji analytics (such as the viral "Dumpster fire" phenomenon), and semantic network mapping can be powerful sources of creative inspiration. By weaving together past research cases—from tracking corporate buzzwords to extracting Archetypal Branding elements from Spotify charts—the presentation showcased how data visualization and text mining can directly fuel modern storytelling.

Butterfly: A Data-Inspired Song

Affiliation: IBM Italia & DensityDesign Lab
June 2018

A unique project at the intersection of technology, data analysis, and musical creativity. In collaboration with IBM Italia and the DensityDesign Research Lab (Politecnico di Milano), this initiative used Artificial Intelligence to inspire "Butterfly," a single by singer-songwriter Alex Uhlmann (frontman of Planet Funk).

IBM Watson (utilizing Discovery News, Natural Language Understanding, and Personality Insights) analyzed three months of trending social media conversations, European news articles, and top Spotify tracks to extract the digital "heartbeat" of societal values and emotions. DensityDesign transformed these insights into evocative data visualizations, revealing "family" and "parenting" as the most deeply debated themes—which ultimately became the core message of the song.

We Are All Nodes, Living in the Network

Affiliation: TEDxVerona February 2014

A TEDx talk introducing the history, methodology, and modern applications of Social Network Analysis (SNA). The presentation traces the mathematical roots of network theory from Leonhard Euler's graph theory to Jacob Levi Moreno's sociogram, before shifting focus to the digital age. It explores how modern SNA is applied to social media data for influencer identification, community mapping, and the semantic analysis of online conversations.

To ground these complex concepts, the talk concludes with a unique case study in educational sciences: demonstrating how kindergarten children intuitively mapped their relational structures using a ball of yarn passed from hand to hand—creating a literal, physical "web of friendship" that beautifully visualizes the core principles of networks.

Vinitaly 2013 on Twitter: Social Network Analysis & Data Journalism

Affiliation: L'Arena di Verona & Dataninja.it June 2013

A data journalism and research project conducted in collaboration with Irene Pasquetto, analyzing the digital footprint and social media chatter during the Vinitaly 2013 international wine exhibition. The study was published as a dedicated feature in the newspaper L'Arena di Verona and showcased within Dataninja.it's research lab.

By applying Social Network Analysis (SNA) methodologies to thousands of tweets containing the organic hashtags #vinitaly and #vinitaly2013, the project mapped the interaction patterns, temporal spikes, and network structures of over 2,600 unique users. The findings tracked the engagement ratios of top active wine bloggers, official institutional accounts (@vinitalytasting), and high-profile industry influencers who drove the global digital conversation.
